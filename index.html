About Me

I am a Ph.D. Candidate in Energy & Mineral Engineering at The Pennsylvania State University, with a minor in Computational Materials. My research focuses on designing and discovering novel electrocatalysts and photocatalysts for green hydrogen production using first-principles DFT simulations, molecular dynamics, and machine learning techniques.

With a strong foundation in Chemical Engineering, Energy Engineering, and Electrochemistry, I am deeply enthusiastic about applying advanced computational tools to solve critical challenges in sustainable energy. My 7+ years of engagement with hydrogen technologies and catalysis drive my commitment to accelerating materials discovery for a cleaner energy future. I am particularly interested in advanced functional materials and their application in energy storage and conversion.
